Features 
	-Micro controller

	-USB to Serial
Terms

	Pins
		-TX
		-RX

		-Digital IO(input and output but only 1,0 language)
		-Analog input(wide range of values instead of just on and off like digital)
			-analogs are connected to analog to digital converter that is built in to arduino(ADC)
			-arduino reads in 1s and 0s so that is why converter is needed
			-ADC range of values is from 0-1023 represented by different Voltages from 0V-5V


		-Pins with a tilde next to them are PWM (pulse width modulated signal pins)
			can turn on and off fast, or 'pulse'

		-VIN pin is an alternate arduino power source that can carry 7V to 12V
			-means 'voltage in' use to have an unregulated power supply
	Arduino extensions
		-sheilds(stackable circuit board)
			-stacks onto arduino and adds specific functionality

	Programming
		arduno IDE uses a modified CPP. Programs written for the arduino are called 'sketch' they have '.ino' extension.

		Sketch program:
			(two required functions)
				void setup(){} and void loop(){}
			
			setup function runs ONCE
			loop runs OVER AND OVER
